Residents of California
Starting on January 1, 2020, the California Consumer Privacy Act (“CCPA") governs how businesses
handle the personal information of California residents. CCPA was amended by the adoption of the
Consumer Privacy Rights Act (“CPRA”) effective March 29, 2023. At PolyBuzz, we are committed to our
subscribers' success, including their compliance efforts with respect to the CCPA. We're here to assist
our subscribers with their efforts to comply with the ССРА.
For PolyBuzz's subscribers, PolyBuzz is a “service provider" under the CCPA. We process personal
information only on behalf of our subscribers, pursuant to written contracts. Those contracts, which are
already in place, permit PolyBuzz to process our subscribers' data (including personal information) only
for the purpose of providing our services. While we necessarily have to process personal information
transmitted through our network, we do not sell that information to third parties.
The CCPA sets forth certain obligations for businesses that “sell" or "share" personal information. Where
we refer to "sell” or “share” (or their variants) in quotes, we are referring to those terms as uniquely
defined in the CCPA.
We may use third-party analytics services and online advertising services that may result in the “sharing”
of online identifiers (e.g., cookie data, IP addresses, device identifiers, general location information, and
usage information) with advertising partners to advertise the Services on other websites. If you or your
authorized agent would like to opt out of our “sharing” of your information for such purposes, you may do
so by go to "settings" on our website or in-app and disable the data sharing. We do not knowingly "sell”
or "share" the personal information of children under 17.

We are committed to complying with the Brazilian General Data Protection Law (LGPD) regarding the
collection, use, and protection of personal data for users located in Brazil. Other than the detailed
explanations of how we handle your personal data in this Privacy Policy, as a Brazilian user, you have the
following rights regarding your personal data under the LGPD: 1) Right to Confirmation: You have the right
to know whether we process any of your personal data. 2) Right to Access: You may request access to
your personal data and receive a copy of the data we hold about you. 3) Right to Rectification: You can
request correction of inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated data. 4) Right to Anonymization, Blocking, or
Deletion: You may request the anonymization, blocking, or deletion of unnecessary or excessive data. 5)
Right to Data Portability: You have the right to request the transfer of your data to another service
provider. 6) Right to Information: You can request information about the entities with whom we have
shared your data. 7) Right to Withdraw Consent: You have the right to withdraw your consent at any time
when data processing is based on consent. 8) Right to Object to Processing: You can object to certain
types of data processing, such as direct marketing or profiling. 9) Right to Review Automated Decisions:
You may request a review of decisions made solely on automated processing, which affect your interests.
